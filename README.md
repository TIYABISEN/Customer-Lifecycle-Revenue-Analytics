# Customer Lifecycle and Revenue Analytics

An end-to-end analytics project that transforms two years of raw e-commerce transaction data into actionable insights on customer retention, purchasing behavior, and revenue performance. Built using a full data pipeline — Python for cleaning and preprocessing, MySQL for relational analysis, and Power BI for interactive reporting.

## Business Context

The dataset represents a UK-based online retailer of occasion gift-ware, covering transactions from December 2009 to December 2011. The analysis addresses three core business questions:

- **Revenue Performance:** How is revenue trending over time, and which markets drive it?
- **Customer Retention:** How well does the business retain customers after their first purchase, and which acquisition periods perform best?
- **Customer Value:** Which customers are most valuable, which are at risk of churning, and how should retention efforts be prioritized?

## Dataset

**Source:** Online Retail II, UCI Machine Learning Repository ([Kaggle mirror](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/mashlyn/online-retail-ii-uci))
**Period:** December 2009 – December 2011
**Scale:** ~1.07M raw transaction records, reduced to ~790K after cleaning

> **Note:** `transactions.csv` exceeds GitHub's file size limit and isn't included; it can be regenerated from the raw dataset using the Python notebook. `orders.csv` and `customers.csv` are included.

## Project Pipeline

**1. Data Preparation (Python)**
Raw transaction data was cleaned to remove duplicates, cancellations, and invalid records, then structured into three relational tables — transactions, orders, and customers — with engineered features including revenue, purchase recency, and cohort assignment.

**2. Analytical Layer (SQL)**
Seven analytical query sections were built in MySQL: executive KPIs, monthly revenue trends, customer acquisition patterns, cohort retention analysis, RFM-based customer segmentation, product performance, and geographic revenue distribution.

**3. Dashboard (Power BI)**
A four-page interactive dashboard was developed:
- **Business Overview** — Revenue KPIs, monthly trends, and top-performing markets
- **Customer Behavior** — New vs. returning customer contribution and purchase frequency distribution
- **Retention Analysis** — Cohort retention heatmap and average retention curves
- **RFM Customer Segments** — Customer segmentation by Recency, Frequency, and Monetary value, with revenue contribution by segment

## Key Insights

- Revenue shows strong seasonal concentration, with peaks in Q4 driven by holiday demand
- A significant share of total revenue is generated by a small base of high-frequency, high-value customers
- Customer retention drops sharply after the first month, highlighting an opportunity for early-lifecycle engagement strategies
- RFM segmentation identifies distinct customer groups — including high-value "Champions" and "At Risk" customers — enabling targeted retention and CRM strategies
